User Stories

International Students

Act as Aisha, a 24 year old international student from Indonesia who just arrived in Australia for 1 month. She is looking for informations about international student communities and events, local services support, any facts and tips living in Australia for international students. Mostly, she uses phone to look for information.

1. What kind of information do you need most when you first arrive in Australia? How you gain information about that?

I needed info about accommodation, transport, mobile SIM, banking, and university services. I got it from university orientation, official websites, Facebook/WhatsApp groups, and Google searches on my phone.

2. How do you usually find out about and join events for international students?

Mostly through university emails, student unions, and international offices. I also use Facebook groups, WhatsApp, Meetup, and Eventbrite to find events.

3. How do you find and network with friends and peers in Australia as an international student?

I connect with classmates, join study groups, and attend uni clubs. Social media groups, volunteering, and part-time jobs also help me meet people.

4. Are you interested in sharing experiences or reading stories from fellow community members? If so, in what format?

Yes, I like reading and sharing experiences. I like quick, interactive conversations where I can ask questions and get immediate answers. My favorite formats are WhatsApp chats, Instagram/Facebook posts, and short videos (Reels/TikTok).

5. What are the challenges using a community website, particularly regarding accessibility or display on mobile devices?

Some sites aren’t mobile-friendly, with small text, slow loading, or messy layouts. Complicated menus and long forms also make it hard to use on a phone.

6. What would make you trust and return to a website as your go-to source of information after arriving in a new country?

Accurate and updated information is most important. I’d return if it’s mobile-friendly, easy to navigate, and includes real student experiences.

7. Is there anything you wish to know before you come in a new country?

Yes, I wish I knew more about cost of living, transport, housing, and health insurance. Tips from other students about daily life and culture would also be very helpful.

International Students

Act as Kevin, a 30 year old international students from Indonesia who just arrived in Australia with his wife. he is looking for informations about international communities and events, local services support, any facts and tips living in Australia. Mostly, he uses phone to look for information.

1. What kind of information do you need most when you first arrive in Australia? How you gain information about that?

When I first arrived, I needed information about accommodation, public transport, healthcare, and student communities. I usually gain this information through my phone—using university websites, social media groups, and apps like Google Maps or Facebook events to find reliable updates and connect with others.

2. How do you usually find out about and join events for international students?

I usually find out about events for international students through my university’s official social media pages, student union announcements, and WhatsApp or Facebook groups for international students. To join, I often just register online through links provided in posts or messages, or sometimes I attend in person if it’s an open event.

3. How do you find and network with friends and peers in Australia as an international student?

I usually network with friends and peers by joining student clubs, attending orientation programs, and participating in university events. I also use social media and messaging apps like WhatsApp or Facebook groups to connect with other international students and exchange tips or arrange meet-ups. Sometimes, casual conversations in class or at local cafes also help me make new friends.

4. Are you interested in sharing experiences or reading stories from fellow community members? If so, in what format?

Yes, I’m definitely interested in both sharing my experiences and reading stories from others. I prefer short posts or chat messages for quick tips, and videos if someone is sharing detailed experiences or guides—it’s easier to follow visually. Sometimes I also enjoy reading personal stories on social media or community forums to learn from real-life experiences.

5. What are the challenges using a community website, particularly regarding accessibility or display on mobile devices?

One challenge is that some community websites aren’t mobile-friendly, so pages may load slowly or the layout gets messy on my phone. Sometimes menus or buttons are hard to tap, or text is too small to read comfortably. Another issue is accessibility—websites may not support screen readers or have poor contrast, which makes it harder for everyone to navigate and find information quickly.

6. What would make you trust and return to a website as your go-to source of information after arriving in a new country?

I would trust a website more if it has up-to-date, accurate information from reliable sources like universities or government services. Clear organization and easy navigation on mobile are important, so I can find what I need quickly. Positive reviews or recommendations from other international students also help. If the website regularly updates events, tips, and local services, I’d keep coming back as my go-to resource.

7. Is there anything you wish to know before you come in a new country?

Before coming to a new country, I wish I knew about practical things like the cost of living, how to open a bank account, healthcare access, and public transport options. It’s also helpful to know about local laws, cultural norms, and student support services. Tips on finding communities, social events, and even mobile apps that make life easier would be great too.

International Students

Act as Budi, a 19 year old international student from Indonesia who just arrived in Australia by himself. This is the first time he is away from home. He is looking for informations about international communities and events, any events for bachelor students, any facts and tips living in Australia. Mostly, he uses phone to look for information.

1. What kind of information do you need most when you first arrive in Australia? How you gain information about that?

When I first arrived in Australia, the information I needed most was about living and studying here, like how to open a bank account, get a SIM card, access healthcare, and find affordable groceries. I also wanted to know about international student communities and events or activities where I could meet new people. I usually find this information through university websites and student portals, social media or messaging groups for international students, apps like Meetup or Eventbrite for events, and by asking other students or friends who have experience living here.

2. How do you usually find out about and join events for international students?

I usually find out about and join events for international students by checking university websites and student portals, which often list orientation sessions, workshops, or social events. I also follow social media and messaging groups, like Facebook or WhatsApp groups for international students, where people share events. Apps like Meetup or Eventbrite are helpful to discover nearby events. Sometimes I see flyers or posters on campus with QR codes to register. After finding an event, I either register online or just attend if it’s free and open.

3. How do you find and network with friends and peers in Australia as an international student?

As an international student in Australia, I usually make friends by joining university clubs, events, and orientation activities, which helps me meet people with similar interests. I also connect with classmates through study groups and classes, which naturally builds friendships. Social media and messaging apps, like Facebook, WhatsApp, or Discord groups for students, are useful for meeting people before and after arriving. Sometimes I meet new friends through meetups, part-time jobs, or volunteering. I usually start by introducing myself and joining shared activities, then follow up online or hang out outside class.

4. Are you interested in sharing experiences or reading stories from fellow community members? If so, in what format?

Yes, I’m interested in both sharing my experiences and reading stories from other community members. I find it helpful to read short posts or articles with tips and personal experiences because I can quickly scroll through them on my phone. Chats or messaging groups are also useful because I can ask questions directly and get real-time advice. Sometimes, short videos are good too, especially if they show practical tips, like navigating campus or cooking simple meals, because it’s easier to understand than just reading.

5. What are the challenges using a community website, particularly regarding accessibility or display on mobile devices?

The biggest challenges I face using a community website on my phone are slow loading times and complicated layouts that are hard to navigate on a small screen. Some websites have menus or buttons that don’t fit well on mobile, making it difficult to find information quickly. Too much text or cluttered pages can also be overwhelming. Sometimes, important features like event registration or messaging don’t work well on mobile, which makes the experience frustrating. I prefer websites that are simple, mobile-friendly, and easy to scroll.

6. What would make you trust and return to a website as your go-to source of information after arriving in a new country?

I would trust and return to a website if it is reliable, up-to-date, and easy to use on my phone. Clear information about living, studying, and events for international students is important, and I like when sources are official or recommended by the university or local community. Features like quick search, organized categories, and simple navigation make it easier to find what I need. I also value user reviews or experiences from other students, because it shows the information is practical and trustworthy. Regular updates and notifications about new events or tips would make me check it often.

7. Is there anything you wish to know before you come in a new country?

Before coming to a new country, I wish I knew more about daily life and practical tips, like how to open a bank account, get a SIM card, use public transport, and find affordable groceries. I’d also like to know about local culture, customs, and social norms to avoid misunderstandings. Information about student communities, events, and ways to make friends would help me feel less lonely. Lastly, knowing safety tips, healthcare access, and emergency contacts would make me feel more prepared and confident.
