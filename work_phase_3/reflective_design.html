Reflective Design

Reflective design focuses on creating products that encourage users to think about their actions and the implications of their choices. This approach aims to foster a deeper connection between users and the product, promoting mindfulness and intentionality in their interactions.

Checklist for Reflective Design

  • 1.4.6 Contrast (Enhanced)
  • Any text element (e.g., paragraph, heading, label) that has a contrast ratio of 2.49 against its background.
  • - Users with low vision, color blindness, or older adults may struggle to read the content.
  • - Poor contrast can lead to eye strain, misinterpretation, or missed information.
  • - It reduces the overall accessibility and usability of your website.
  • - For large text that is at least 18 point or at least 14 point when bold, adjust the color of the text or its background to give at least a 3 to 1 contrast ratio.
  • - For body text that is less than 18 point or less than 14 point when bold, adjust the color of the text or its background to give at least a 4.5 to 1 contrast ratio.

It is unclear whether the keyboard focus indicator is visible when using CSS declaration for 'border' or 'outline'

  • 2.4.7 Focus visible

Any interactive element (e.g., buttons, links, form inputs) that:

  • - Has custom styles applied using border, outline, or box-shadow.
  • - May have had the default focus styles removed or overridden.
  • - Users who navigate using a keyboard (e.g., due to motor disabilities or preference) may not know which element is currently active.
  • - This can lead to confusion, navigation errors, or inability to complete tasks on the site.
  • - Use the default focus indicator for the platform.
  • - Adjust the CSS styling to make the keyboard focus highly visible.
  • - Use interface components that are highlighted by the user agent when they receive focus.
  • - Use scripting to change the background color or border of the element to make it visually distinct when it receives focus
