Implementation Rationale

Alignment with Client and User Needs

My website is designed with international students in mind, aiming to address their key needs as outlined in the user stories. To ensure accurate and updated information, each article includes its source and publication date, helping users trust the content and stay informed with the latest developments.

To support interactive conversation, the website features a thread-like message board where users can ask questions, share insights, and engage with the community. While features like commenting, liking, and sharing are not yet available, they are part of the future roadmap to enhance interactivity and real-time engagement.

Through the message board, users also gain access to real student experiences, as discussions often include personal stories, tips, and testimonials from fellow international students. This creates a more authentic and relatable environment.

Although the website is still a work in progress, I‚Äôve made efforts to ensure it is easy to use on mobile, allowing users to navigate quickly and with minimal effort, even on smaller screens.

My website aligns with the goals of the Connect Communities Initiative (CCI) by supporting both community building and practitioner engagement through its features:
  • ‚Ä¢ Building Community Connections
    Through the Communities and Networks page, users can explore and connect with various student groups and support networks.
  • ‚Ä¢ Supporting Practitioners
    Articles may include events and information about potential activities and opportunities, helping practitioners stay informed and engaged.

Accessibility and Ethical Considerations

Perceiveable

1.3.2 Meaningful Sequence
The contents are delivered in a meaningful flow to help or guide user to use the website. For example in Article Page, the content begin with search box. It helps user to go directly to certain or specific article that they already now. Below the search box, I put a collection of Articles for user who don't now what articles they want to get or for user who just casually strolling the website.

Operable

2.4.2 Page Titled
Each webpage have already applied title based on the context of the webpage

2.4.4 Link Purpose (In Context)
Applied in the Communities and Networks page, section List of Community. In there, the purpose of each card is to open the specific community landing page.

Understandable

3.1.5 Reading Level
Since this is a website for International Students whose English is not their mother language, I did not use any scientific or difficult words. If I have to use it in the article, I might put a little footnote to give a context for non english speaker to make it more Understandable.

3.2.3 Consistent Navigation
I applied the same structure for my main webpage; Home, Articles, and Communities & Networks. It begin with a hero banner, title of the page, and then the content.

3.3.1 Error Identification
I will have error identification for any form feature to help user recognize error and handle the it. However, right now, I did not implement it in my website yet.

Challenges and Strategies

One of the main challenges I faced during this project was understanding and implementing APIs. Initially, I struggled to grasp the concept of how APIs work and how to choose the right endpoints that matched the content and functionality of my website. Making the API function correctly was also a significant hurdle. To overcome these challenges, I used Generative AI tools to deepen my understanding of APIs and their practical applications. I asked for guidance on how specific APIs could be integrated into my website and sought help in identifying suitable endpoints. Additionally, I used AI assistance to review my JavaScript and API-related code, helping me identify and fix errors more efficiently.
